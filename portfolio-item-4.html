Fuel Solution for Rental Cars

Company: Sixt Rent A Car
Date: April, 2018

My first assignment as a project manager was to manage the installation of an on-site fuel tank and management system for Sixt Rent A Car's new Denver branch. The project was implemented in two major phases. First, we installed a mobile fuel tank for the temporary branch to use during its first few months. Meanwhile, our vendor Indigo Engergy would build a permanent on-site 12,000-gallon tank at the new branch. Based on the anticipated fleet size, fuel consumption, and historical company data, the project was projected to produce $56K annual savings after three-year payback period.

Project Description

Traditionally rental car companies have two options when it comes to refueling the vehicles. Many of the on airport branches have a fuel pump on site that is shared by multiple rental car companies. Downtown branches typically use a neighborhood gas station that is within a few blocks of the branch.

Because the closest gas station was 2 ½ miles away and had a significant markup based on its proximity to the airport, I was asked by management to look into fueling options for our new Denver location. My first step was to write a business case and present an analysis of our options.
